About the Lab

The Cellular Architectures Laboratory (CAL) investigates the intersection of computational design, structural mechanics, and digital fabrication. Our work explores how cellular and lattice-based geometries can be engineered to achieve high structural performance with minimal material — bridging architecture, engineering, and material science.

Through parametric modeling, form-finding, and topology optimization, the lab develops methods for designing efficient shell and lattice structures, and translates these computational models into fabricable, physical systems.

Director

Dr. Mostafa Akbari

Assistant Professor of Architecture · Texas A&M University

Dr. Mostafa Akbari leads the Cellular Architectures Laboratory in the Department of Architecture at Texas A&M University. His research focuses on computational design, structural form-finding, and the digital fabrication of high-performance cellular and shell structures.

He teaches courses in computational architecture, parametric modeling, and structural design, integrating research-driven methods into design education.

Research Areas
FORM-FINDING

Structural Form-Finding

Computational approaches to efficient structural form — funicular geometries, load paths, and equilibrium.

GEOMETRY

Cellular & Lattice Geometry

Shellular surfaces, meshes, and polyhedral cellular systems as the basis for lightweight structures.

OPTIMIZATION

Topology Optimization

Algorithmic and evolutionary methods for optimizing structural and spatial performance.

FABRICATION

Digital Fabrication

Translating computational models to physical artifacts — fabrication-aware design and toolpath generation.

MATERIAL

Material Computation

Designing material distribution and behavior into form, coupling geometry with mechanical response.

ANALYSIS

Structural Analysis

Evaluating performance through simulation — stress, deformation, and optimization workflows.
